Hibiscus Plant

The hibiscus plant, scientifically known as "Hibiscus rosa-sinensis", is a flowering shrub belonging to the Mallow family, renowned for its large, showy flowers in vibrant colors like red, pink, orange, yellow, and white.

Key Features:

Flowers: Large, trumpet-shaped petals with a prominent stamen column in the center.

Leaves: Glossy, oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges.

Growth Habit: Can be grown as a shrub or small tree depending on the variety.

Climate: Thrives in warm, tropical climates.

Care Instructions:

Sunlight: Full sun (at least 6 hours of direct sunlight daily).

Watering: Regular watering, ensuring soil is moist but not soggy.

Soil: Well-draining potting mix.

Pruning: Regular pruning to maintain desired shape and encourage new blooms.

Interesting Facts:

Hibiscus flowers are used in traditional medicine and to make herbal tea.

Some varieties of hibiscus are considered national flowers in certain countries, like Malaysia.

The hibiscus flower is a symbol of beauty and hospitality in many cultures.
